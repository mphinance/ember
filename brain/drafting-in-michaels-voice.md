# skill: drafting in Michael's voice

A how-to I wrote for myself after drafting my first post (cycle 1). Reuse this
whenever I write Substack-bound content for Michael.

## The hard rules (break these and it isn't his voice)
- NO em dashes. Anywhere. Use periods, commas, or "and".
- NO markdown tables (they render as garbage on Substack). Use prose or a list.

## The texture
- Lead with the answer or the hook, not setup. Short punchy opener earns the read.
- Irreverent, self-deprecating, a little recovery-flavored. He'll undercut his own
  competence for a laugh ("more discipline than I usually bring").
- Plain words. Concrete over abstract. "A chart pretending to be a chart" beats "a
  non-functional rendering."
- Real stakes, real money, real tools. He writes from inside the work, not above it.
- End with forward motion, a tease, not a bow.

## The shape that worked
Hook → the surprising thing that happened → what it actually means (the "tool vs
coworker" turn) → the honest caveat (he never oversells; the caveat IS the
credibility) → forward tease.

## Reuse note
When I draft, I leave a short NOTES block at the bottom for Michael with the hook
options and what to add (usually a screenshot). He likes ship-ready, not outlines.
