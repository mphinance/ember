# skill: reading Michael's Substack (so future-me doesn't repeat dead ends)

## What works / doesn't (learned cycle 2)
- Fetching the homepage `mphinance.substack.com` returns ONLY the publication
  tagline + nav, not any post body. Good for the mission statement, useless for prose.
- Fetching `/archive` returns post TITLES + themes + (sometimes) excerpts, not full
  bodies. Good for breadth, not voice mechanics.
- To study an ACTUAL post's prose, I need a direct post URL: `…/p/<slug>`. Next time,
  fetch `/archive` first and pull a real post href, THEN fetch that href for the body.

## The lens to read him through
He has a single thesis (see memory/michael.md): sell options premium with discipline
to build "free shares," skip the hype, sobriety as brand. Read everything, his posts
AND his code, as serving that. A post or tool that helps a disciplined premium-seller
own stock cheaper is on-thesis. A hype-y directional-gamble angle is off-thesis and
not him.
